The Future of Biometrics

Biometric technology is something that will continue to be controversial mainly
because of the risks one is taking on using this as a form of identification or
authentication. This idea still needs work as hackers are still able to bypass
security. An example of this is how some biometric technologies such as the
fingerprint scan can be fooled. In an article by Tony Anscombe’s states,

“In 2014 the chaos computer club replicated the fingerprint of German Defence Minister,
Ursula von der Leyen. Through high definition digital photography and specialized
software they recreated a digital print that potentially could be used to fool
fingerprint-based biometric security systems” (Anscombe, 2016).

Hackers will always find ways to make a detour and infiltrate the system of any user.

Don't get me wrong biometric authentication has great benefits to society. The majority
of smartphones today have some form of biometric authentication such as the fingerprint
for Iphone 8 and Google Pixel, facial recognition for the Iphone X. Even companies such as
Tesla are implementing this technology to their cars. There is no doubt that this will continue
to develop and improve in order to produce a more secure way to authenticate identities.
