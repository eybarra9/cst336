Trends in Biometrics

Biometrics have come a long way since the ancient era of human civilization.
Recent trends today include:

  • Ear: Identification by the shape of the ear
  • Eyes(Iris): The use of eyes to identify the person
  • Face: Analysis of facial features and patterns to authenticate
  • Fingerprint: The ridges of fingers are unique to each person
  • Typing: The unique patterns found of a person's typing behavior
  • Voice: Unique pitch, frequency, and tone of voice to authenticate

All of these have been some of the ways corporations have used biometrics and
have implemented them to modern technology such as a fingerprint scanner on a mobile
device. Even more recently is behavior biometrics. An example is keystroke dynamics where
the rhythm in which an indivdual types on a keyboard is used to authenticate an identity.
